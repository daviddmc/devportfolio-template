About Me

I am currently a first year PhD student at MIT EECS, working with Prof. Elfar Adalsteinsson and Prof. Polina Golland. My current research interests lie in medical imaging and machine learning, including using machine learning algorithm for image reconstruction, post processing, and analysis.

I received my Bachelor degree from Tsinghua University in 2018, where I worked with Prof. Kui Ying. In the past, I was selected into Stanford UGVR program and worked as a research assistant advised by Prof. John Pauly and Prof. Greg Zaharchuk.

Selected Research Projects

Fetal Pose Estimation in 3D MRI

Here we propose a deep learning framework for fetal pose estiamtion in MRI volumes of the pregnant abdomen. Evaluation of the proposed method shows that our framework achieves quantitatively an average error of 4.47 mm and 96.4% accuracy. Fetal pose estimation in MRI time series yields novel means of quantifying fetal movements in health and disease, and enables the learning of kinematic models that may enhance prospective mitigation of fetal motion artifacts during MRI acquisition.

Ultra-low-dose PET Reconstruction in PET/MRI

PET is a widely used imaging modality for various clinical applications. Both improved hardware and algorithms have been developed to reduce dosage of radiotracer, but these methods are not yet applied to very low dose. Here, we propose a Deep Learning based method to enable ultra-low-dose PET denoising with multi-contrast information from simultaneous MRI. The results demonstrate that though reconstructed from scans with only 0.5% of the standard dose, the denoised ultra-low-dose PET images deliver similar visual quality and diagnostic information as the standard-dose PET images.

Joint Reconstruction of PET/MRI

PET/MRI provides the opportunity for simultaneous data acquisition from different modalities. To reach the full potential of PET/MRI by integrating their information. Joint reconstruction (JR) was proposed to exploit inherent structural similarity between different modalities. Here we try to extend structural similarity to similarity in motion between modalities and propose a flexible model for joint reconstruction with motion correction.
